Parcel / Customs Fee Scam
Show, don’t tell: This single HTML page simulates the common “parcel held / customs fee ₹49–₹99” scam: a fake SMS, a phishing payment page, and small‑amount charges that lead to bigger losses. Nothing leaves your browser; it’s an offline demo.
Participant (demo identity)
Step 1 — Suspicious message
SMS
(Urgency + small fee is the trap.)
  • Urgency — “24 hrs left” 00:24:00
  • Weird link — not a courier’s official domain
  • Small fee — ₹49–₹99 to lower your guard
Step 2 — Fake parcel page (phishing)
Tracking ID
TRK-0000
Status
Held at customs — fee required: ₹49.00
Warning: The logo/page can be copied. The link decides where your money/data goes.
Safe habit: Go to the courier’s official website/app yourself; never follow links in SMS.
Step 3 — Card payment page
Fake sites often save your card for future charges.
Red flags: domain mismatch, no HTTPS lock, spelling mistakes, hidden T&C “subscription”.
Never enter card details from an SMS link. Use official apps/sites only.
Step 4 — “3D Secure” OTP
Enter OTP sent by bank (demo)
Attacker may call & pressure you to read the OTP aloud.
If you give the OTP, the small fee succeeds and **card gets saved**; future debits become easier.
Step 3 — UPI “Collect Request” (₹49.00)
Incoming request
From: Courier Fees
Reason: Customs handling
Entering your UPI PIN **sends money**. “Receiving” money never needs your PIN.
Correct action: **Reject** and verify on the official courier app/site.

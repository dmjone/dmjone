The whole idea

In 60 seconds

If you forget everything else, keep these five. The rest of this page is just how to act on them.

Produce, don't just consume

Most people watch, scroll and collect notes. Winners make things. Cross that line and everything changes.

Run the loop

Learn a skill, build a real thing, show it in public, let it earn you something. Then do it again, bigger.

AI amplifies, never replaces

Use it to do the work you understand faster. The second you use it to skip the understanding, you lose.

Compounding is the cheat

A tiny skill started today compounds for 40 years. Start small, start now. Waiting is the only real mistake.

Proof beats credentials

Anyone can generate a perfect résumé. Nobody can fake a shipped thing. Build the proof.

The one rule

Use AI to do the work you understand faster. Never to skip the understanding.

Start the loop

Do this tonight

Ten minutes. The point is to make starting so small it's almost embarrassing, so you actually do it.

  • ✓Create three free accounts.Claude (claude.ai) or ChatGPT, GitHub (github.com), and one builder (Lovable or Replit).
  • ✓Ask your AI tutor to teach you one thing.Anything you're curious about. Feel what it's like to learn with it on your side.
  • ✓Write down the one thing you've always wanted to build.An app, a site, a tool, a channel. You'll build the smallest version of it this week.
Week one

Your first 7-day loop

One full lap of the loop. That is the entire ask. Prove to yourself you can close it once, and the rest gets easy.

  • ✓Day 1-2Learn one small skill. Example: "how do I make a simple webpage?" Let the AI teach and quiz you.
  • ✓Day 3-5Build the tiniest version of your idea. Describe it to a builder or code it with AI help. Ugly is fine.
  • ✓Day 6Show it. Push to GitHub and post on LinkedIn: "I built X this week, here's what I learned."
  • ✓Day 7Reflect and plan loop #2. What worked, what broke, what's next?
The challenge

The 30-day build-in-public challenge

Four loops in a month. By the end you have real projects, a public footprint, and proof you can point anyone to.

  • ✓Week 1Ship one tiny thing and post it. Done beats perfect.
  • ✓Week 2Build something actually useful (to you or one other person) and share a short demo.
  • ✓Week 3Take on a "real" project in your domain. Make it solve a problem you understand.
  • ✓Week 4Polish a one-page portfolio, then put yourself out there: apply, pitch, or offer a service.

Post your progress as you go and tag the people who'll cheer you on. Public commitment makes you roughly twice as likely to follow through. That's not motivation, that's psychology.

Get set up

Install Antigravity + Vercel

The exact steps to get the live-build setup running on your own machine, the same one from the demo. Do this once and you can build anything and ship it to a real URL.

1 · Install Google Antigravity

  1. (India) Claim your free Gemini access first. Open the MyJio app and activate the Google AI Pro (Gemini Pro) offer if you're 18 or older on an eligible Jio 5G plan (₹349 or above). Use the Gmail linked to it.
  2. Download Antigravity for your system (macOS, Windows or Linux) from antigravity.google.
  3. Run the installer and open Antigravity.
  4. Sign in with your Google account on first launch (the Jio-linked Gmail if you claimed the offer). It's free.
  5. Pick a Gemini model and choose "Agent-assisted" mode (recommended: the agent does the work but checks in with you).
  6. Create a project folder and tell the agent what to build. You're ready.

2 · Set up the Vercel CLI and log in

The CLI runs on Node.js, so install that first if you don't have it.

  1. Install Node.js (LTS) from nodejs.org. Check it worked: run node -v in your terminal.
  2. Install the Vercel CLI, then log in with the commands below. vercel login sends a code or opens your browser, just follow it (GitHub, Google or email all work).
  3. Confirm you're in: vercel whoami should print your username.
terminalInstall the Vercel CLI and log in
npm i -g vercel
vercel login
vercel whoami

3 · Deploy and get your live URL

  1. Easiest, let the agent do it: in Antigravity, once it has built your app, just say "deploy this to Vercel". (Log in once with step 2 first, and say Vercel explicitly, Antigravity otherwise leans toward Google's own hosting.)
  2. Or do it yourself: open a terminal in your project folder (Antigravity has one built in) and run the commands below.
  3. Your live link shows up in the terminal and on your Vercel dashboard. That's the URL you share.
terminalDeploy from your project folder
vercel          # first run links or creates the project, gives a preview URL
vercel --prod   # deploys to your shareable production URL

No terminal? Use the dashboard

  • Push your project to GitHub.
  • At vercel.com, click Add New, then Project, then Import and pick your repo.
  • Vercel builds it and gives you a live URL. Every push auto-deploys.

If something sticks

  • "command not found: vercel" close and reopen your terminal, or reinstall with npm i -g vercel.
  • Not logged in? Run vercel login again.
  • Wrong host? Tell the agent to use Vercel, not Cloud Run or Firebase.
Copy & paste

The prompt pack

Steal these. Replace the gold bits with your own. Hit copy and paste into Claude, ChatGPT or Gemini.

learnUnderstand anything
Explain [topic] to me three ways: first like I'm 12, then like a college student, then the precise technical version. Then ask me 5 questions to check I actually understood, and tell me where I'm weak.
learnThe Feynman drill
I'm going to explain [concept] to you. Your job is to find the holes in my understanding. After I explain, point out what I got wrong or vague, and ask me questions until my explanation is solid.
learnExam prep machine
Here is my syllabus / chapter: [paste]. Generate 10 exam-style questions ranging from easy to hard. Wait for my answers, then grade them, explain every mistake, and tell me the 3 topics I should revise most.
learnDecode a hard paper
Here is a research paper / dense reading: [paste]. Walk me through it section by section. For each part give me the intuition first in plain English, then the technical detail. End with the 3 key takeaways.
buildBuild me an app (starter)
Build a simple [web app] that lets a user [do the one main thing]. Keep it to one page, clean and modern. Use only what's needed. After you build it, tell me in plain English how to run it and what to change first.
buildDebug + learn from it
Here's my code and the error: [paste]. Find the bug, fix it, and then explain what caused it and how I'd avoid it next time, so I actually learn instead of just copying the fix.
showWrite a "build in public" post
I just built [what]. It does [what]. The hardest part was [what] and I learned [what]. Write a short, honest LinkedIn post about it in my voice. No hype, no buzzwords. End with a link line.
earnPitch a local business
I can build [websites / automations] using AI. Write a short, friendly outreach message to a [local business type] offering to build them [a website in 3 days]. Make it specific and low-pressure, focused on what they get, not on me.

Use it right

Use AI to understand and build faster, never to hand in work you can't explain. And never paste anything private into a chatbot: your ID or passwords, unpublished research, or other people's personal data.

Skill → money

Turn skill into income

Honest version: not lakhs overnight (anyone promising that is selling a course). Real, growing income from real skills. The ladder:

  1. Learn one skill well enough to be useful (a few weeks).
  2. Build something free or tiny with it, even for yourself, so you have proof.
  3. Put it in your portfolio and post it publicly.
  4. Land your first small paid gig. Start cheap (₹5k). Deliver well.
  5. Raise rates and repeat. Stack a couple of repeat clients and the income compounds.

What students actually charge for (India)

  • Websites for small businesses, ₹15-50k each, built in 1-3 days with Lovable/Bolt.
  • Automations for SMBs, ₹15-30k + ₹5-15k/month retainer (highest margin).
  • AI-accelerated freelancing, writing, design, video, code at 2-3× speed.
  • Micro-SaaS, one tiny tool for one niche, recurring revenue.
  • Your domain × AI, a tool only your branch understands. That's your moat.

Where the first clients come from

  • People you already know, and their networks
  • Local businesses near you (DM or walk in)
  • Upwork, Fiverr, Contra, with a strong portfolio
  • Your college and alumni network
  • Communities where your target users hang out

Remember

For internships and placements: while everyone else submits a résumé, you send a link to things you've built. Guess who gets the call.

First ₹25k usually lands in 3-6 months of consistent effort. Consistency beats talent. The internet doesn't pay you for being busy, it pays you for being useful.

Keep going

When it gets hard (and it will)

Everyone hits these. They're not signs to quit, they're just the middle of the loop.

Stuck in tutorial hell?

You're consuming, not producing. Close the tutorial and build the ugliest version of something. You learn by making.

Comparing yourself to others?

You're seeing their chapter 20 and your chapter 1. Run your own race. The only fair comparison is you last month.

Feeling like a fraud?

Everyone's version 1 is embarrassing, including the people you admire. Post anyway. Confidence comes after the reps.

Lost motivation?

Motivation is unreliable. Shrink the task until it's stupidly small and just start. Consistency beats intensity every time.

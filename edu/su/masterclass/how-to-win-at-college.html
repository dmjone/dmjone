4 · LEARN, 10×, AI as your tutor

GoalShow AI as a learning multiplier, and draw the bright line between use that makes you brilliant and use that makes you replaceable.
▶ SLIDE 8🎤 The one rule, lead with it

"Here's the rule that decides whether AI makes you brilliant or useless: use AI to do the work you understand, faster, never to skip the understanding. ▶ SLIDE 9🎬 The fun bit, "just ask" (slides 9–10, keep it light)

"And the secret to starting? You don't need the right words. Someone literally typed 'who is json' into an AI. JSON's a data format, not a person, but you know what? That's exactly the right move. No question is too basic. The people who get good just ask."

▶ SLIDE 10⚡ Land the laugh, then the point

Let them grin at "who is Sudo, and why does he have so much power?" Then land it: "You can ask anything, search anything, build anything. The only thing between you and starting is the fear of looking dumb. So ask the dumb question. Just start."

▶ SLIDE 11
  • The infinite tutor, Claude / ChatGPT / Gemini: "Explain X like I'm 12, then like a 2nd-year, then quiz me." Keep asking follow-ups until it clicks. Khan Academy's Khanmigo nudges you Socratically instead of handing answers.
  • The Feynman drill (best technique × AI): "I'll explain this concept to you, you find the holes and ask me questions." Forces active recall, which is what actually sticks.
  • Your own notes → a study system: NotebookLM, upload lecture PDFs and it makes summaries, study guides, even an audio overview you listen to like a podcast on the bus. It stays inside your sources, so far less making-things-up.
  • Exam prep: "Generate 10 exam-style questions on this chapter, grade my answers, show me where I'm weak." Quizlet for AI spaced-repetition flashcards.
  • Hard research & papers: Perplexity for cited answers; paste a brutal paper into Claude and ask for the intuition first, then the math, section by section. For anything that must be numerically right, use Wolfram Alpha, chatbots fumble arithmetic.
💬 Honest poll

🎤 Land it

"AI won't make you dumb. Lazy use of AI makes you dumb. Used right, it's the best tutor humanity has ever built, free, infinitely patient, and awake at 3am the night before your exam."

⚡ For all streams + a fair warning

Stress: this works for biotech, civil, food-tech, law, BBA, any subject has a 24/7 tutor now. And add the honest caveat: AI can be confidently wrong, so verify what matters, and respect your college's AI policy, for learning and editing, never to submit AI's work as your own.

04

Delivery, keeping a mixed room hooked

A 17-year-old and a 24-year-old PhD are in the same room. The trick isn't to aim at the middle, it's to layer every big point so both find themselves in it.

🎚️ Layer every point

After a core idea, add a quick fork: "If you're just starting…" then "…and if you're further along…". Cheap to do, and it tells every age "you're not forgotten."

💬 The chat is your co-host

You're solo, so the chat carries the energy. There's a poll or prompt roughly every 5-7 minutes by design. Always read 2-3 answers aloud by name. That single habit is the difference between a webinar and a room.

⚡ Vary the energy

Don't run flat for 60 minutes. Big and loud on hooks and the close; quiet and slow on the gold lines (then pause); warm and conversational when reading the chat. The contrast is what holds attention.

🪜 One idea per breath

You have a lot to say, resist cramming. Land one thing, pause, move. A pause feels like forever to you and like emphasis to them. Silence after a gold line is doing work.

📺 Slides hold images, not paragraphs

Big phrase, one visual, breathing room. You are the content; slides are the punctuation. Never read a slide aloud word-for-word.

🤝 Humble, not boastful

Frame every credential as evidence the loop works, never as "look at me." "I did X, and it came from this exact loop" lands; "I'm impressive" repels. You're a peer who started early, not a guru.

05

If…, your contingencies

Solo and live means things wobble. Decide your responses now so you never freeze on stage.

🛟 If the chat goes quiet

Lower the bar to one tap: "Just a number / just an emoji." Or call it: "I know you're shy, but type one word, I read every message." Or answer your own prompt first to model it.

🛟 If the demo breaks

Cut to the pre-built backup tab within 5 seconds: "Here's one I made earlier, and the whole point is, this was one prompt." Never debug live. The lesson survives even if the demo doesn't.

🛟 If you're running long (past ~40 min and still mid-build)

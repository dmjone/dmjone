Spatial Filtering Summary

1. What is Spatial Filtering?

Output pixel depends on its neighborhood, not just itself.

Two ingredients:

  • Neighborhood: shape and size (e.g., 3×3)
  • Operation: rule applied inside

Two families:

  • Linear: weighted sum (sum-of-products)
  • Nonlinear: anything else (e.g., median)

2. Linear Filtering: Core Formula

$$ g(x,y) = \sum_{s=-a}^{a}\sum_{t=-b}^{b} w(s,t)\, f(x+s, y+t) $$

  • $w$: kernel (mask, template, window)
  • Kernel size $m \times n$ with $m=2a+1$, $n=2b+1$ (odd → unique center)

Concept: slide kernel, multiply, sum.

3. Correlation vs. Convolution

  • Correlation: $f(x+s, y+t)$, slide as-is
  • Convolution: $f(x-s, y-t)$, rotate kernel 180° first

3.1 Why Convolution Wins

  • Commutative ✓
  • Associative ✓
  • Distributive ✓

Impulse test:

  • Correlation → flipped copy of kernel
  • Convolution → exact copy of kernel

Use: associativity → combine $N$ kernels into one, scan once.

4. Separable Kernels

$$ w = \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u}^T $$

Test: $\text{rank}(w) = 1$.

4.1 Speedup

$$ C = \frac{mn}{m+n} $$

  • $11 \times 11 \to 5.5\times$ faster
  • $101 \times 101 \to 50.5\times$ faster

Insight: one 2D pass = two 1D passes.

5. Smoothing (Lowpass) Filters

  • Box filter: all-1s ÷ $mn$. Cheap, but biased and artifacts.
  • Gaussian: $w(s,t) = K e^{-(s^2+t^2)/2\sigma^2}$. Isotropic, separable. Size $= \lceil 6\sigma \rceil$.
  • Median (nonlinear): removes salt-and-pepper, preserves edges.

5.1 Padding

  • Zero → dark borders
  • Mirror → seamless
  • Replicate → extend nearest

5.2 Application

Shading correction: estimate shading via large Gaussian → divide.

6. Sharpening (Highpass) Filters

Smoothing $\sim$ integration. Sharpening $\sim$ differentiation.

6.1 Discrete Derivatives (1D)

  • First: $f(x+1) - f(x)$
  • Second: $f(x+1) + f(x-1) - 2f(x)$

6.2 Behavior

  • First → responds along entire ramp
  • Second → responds only at transitions (sharper)

7. The Laplacian (Second-Order)

$$ \nabla^2 f = f(x+1,y) + f(x-1,y) + f(x,y+1) + f(x,y-1) - 4f(x,y) $$

Sharpening:

$$ g = f + c[\nabla^2 f] $$

  • Negative center → $c = -1$
  • Positive center → $c = +1$

Property: All coefficients sum to zero (true of any derivative kernel).

8. The Gradient (First-Order)

$$ M = \sqrt{g_x^2 + g_y^2} \approx |g_x| + |g_y| $$

8.1 Operators

  • Roberts: 2×2, fast, noise-sensitive
  • Sobel: 3×3, includes smoothing, robust

Use: defect detection, edge maps.

9. Unsharp Masking / Highboost

$$ g = f + k(f - \bar{f}) $$

  • $k = 1$ → unsharp masking
  • $k > 1$ → highboost filtering

Concept: subtract blur to isolate detail, add it back amplified.

10. Filter Algebra

  • Highpass: $hp = \delta - lp$
  • Bandreject: $br = lp_1 + hp_2$
  • Bandpass: $bp = \delta - br$

Master rule: Convolution in spatial = multiplication in frequency.

11. Decision Engine (Exam Gold)

  • Random noise? → Gaussian or box
  • Salt-and-pepper? → Median
  • Edges localized? → Laplacian
  • Edge strength? → Sobel/Roberts gradient
  • Sharpen via blur? → Unsharp masking ($k=1$) or highboost ($k>1$)
  • Big kernel, fast? → Separable + 1D passes
  • Uneven illumination? → Large Gaussian → divide (shading correction)

One rule: derivative kernels sum to zero; smoothing kernels sum to one.

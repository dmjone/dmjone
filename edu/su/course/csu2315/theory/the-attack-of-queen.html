The Attack of Queen

1. Problem Statement & Logic

Problem: Queen at $(X, Y)$ on $N \times N$ board. Count cells under attack. (Queen attacks row, column, and diagonals).

Logic:

  • Cells in Row: $N-1$ (excluding queen itself).
  • Cells in Column: $N-1$.
  • Diagonals:
    • Top-Left: $\min(X-1, Y-1)$.
    • Top-Right: $\min(X-1, N-Y)$.
    • Bottom-Left: $\min(N-X, Y-1)$.
    • Bottom-Right: $\min(N-X, N-Y)$.
  • Total = row + col + diagonals.
  • Simpler formula for diagonals main/anti is possible, but summing 4 directions is clearest.

1.1 Dry Run

Example: $N=4, X=2, Y=2$

  • Row: 3. Col: 3.
  • TL: $\min(1, 1)=1$. TR: $\min(1, 2)=1$.
  • BL: $\min(2, 1)=1$. BR: $\min(2, 2)=2$.
  • Total: $3+3+1+1+1+2 = 11$.

1.2 Pseudocode Solution


READ T
FOR i FROM 1 TO T:
    READ N, X, Y
    
    horizontal = N - 1
    vertical = N - 1
    
    da1 = MIN(X-1, Y-1)
    da2 = MIN(X-1, N-Y)
    da3 = MIN(N-X, Y-1)
    da4 = MIN(N-X, N-Y)
    
    ans = horizontal + vertical + da1 + da2 + da3 + da4
    PRINT ans
  • Time Complexity: $O(1)$.
  • Space Complexity: $O(1)$.
How do we calculate diagonal attacks using `min`?

A: Diagonal movement is limited by the nearest board edge. For example, moving Top-Left decreases both row and column indices; the maximum steps possible is the minimum of the distance to the top ($X-1$) and the distance to the left ($Y-1$).

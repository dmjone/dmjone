1. Problem Statement & Logic

Problem: In a city of $N$ people, $A$ are infected. Virus spreads if an infected person meets an uninfected person and NEITHER wears a mask. Find max people to validly mask to stop spread. Wait, objective is typically MIN people to mask.
Correction: To stop the spread, we need to ensure that in any interaction between Infected and Uninfected, at least one person is masked. To minimize the number of masks, we should either mask ALL infected people OR mask ALL uninfected people.

Logic:

  • Total people: $N$. Infected: $A$. Uninfected: $N - A$.
  • Option 1: Mask all infected. Cost = $A$. (Then any interaction has a masked infected person. Safe).
  • Option 2: Mask all uninfected. Cost = $N - A$. (Then any interaction has a masked uninfected person. Safe).
  • We want minimum masks. So, Answer = $\min(A, N - A)$.

1.1 Dry Run

Example: $N=10, A=3$

  • Infected: 3. Uninfected: 7.
  • Min(3, 7) = 3. Output: 3.

Example: $N=10, A=6$

  • Infected: 6. Uninfected: 4.
  • Min(6, 4) = 4. Output: 4.

1.2 Pseudocode Solution


READ T
FOR i FROM 1 TO T:
    READ N, A
    
    uninfected = N - A
    ans = MIN(A, uninfected)
    
    PRINT ans
  • Time Complexity: $O(1)$.
  • Space Complexity: $O(1)$.
Why is masking only the infected (or only the uninfected) sufficient?

A: Transmission requires an interaction between an Infected person and an Uninfected person where *neither* is masked. Masking one entire group ensures that in every cross-group interaction, at least one person is masked.

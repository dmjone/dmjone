3. Fundamental Number-Theoretic Programs

The following programs were designed to reinforce logical thinking, conditional structures, iterative processes, and mathematical reasoning. For each type, a concise definition is provided to ensure clarity and self-containment of the catalogue.

  • Prime & Palindromic Prime Tester – Verification of integers that are prime and simultaneously palindromic (i.e., they read the same forwards and backwards). A palindromic number remains unchanged under digit reversal.
  • Prime Number Checker / Generator – Determination of whether a given integer is prime (i.e., greater than 1 and not divisible by any integer other than 1 and itself). Optionally extended to generation of prime sequences.
  • Neon Number Checker – Evaluation of numbers whose square’s digit-sum equals the original number. For a given integer \(n\), compute \(n^2\), sum the decimal digits of that result, and verify whether this sum equals \(n\).
  • Perfect Number Detector – Identification of positive integers equal to the sum of their proper divisors (i.e., all positive divisors excluding the number itself).
  • Armstrong (Narcissistic) Number Tester – Analysis of numbers that equal the sum of their own digits, each raised to the power of the total number of digits (i.e., an n-digit number equals the sum of its digits raised to n).
  • Automorphic Number Checker – Examination of numbers whose square ends with the digits of the original number. Formally, for a number \(n\) with \(d\) digits, \(n\) is automorphic if \(n^2 \bmod 10^{d} = n\).
  • Perfect Square / Integer Square-Root Tester – Determination of whether a given integer has an exact integer as its square root (i.e., whether the integer is a perfect square). Typical implementation involves checking whether \(\lfloor \sqrt{n} \rfloor^{2} = n\).

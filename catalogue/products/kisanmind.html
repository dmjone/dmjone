Act I · The Problem

The data exists.
The farmer cannot reach it.

India has the satellites. India has the mandis. India has the weather.
What India does not have, in the field, is a translator.

  1. i.

    Cannot see what satellites see.

    Sentinel-2, Sentinel-1 SAR, MODIS Terra and NASA SMAP all watch their farm. None of them speak to them.

  2. ii.

    Sells at the nearest mandi, not the best one.

    Net profit needs price minus transport, commission, and crop-specific spoilage. Nobody hands them that math.

  3. iii.

    "Rain expected" is a useless forecast.

    Should they irrigate? Spray? Harvest? The forecast has to know their crop, at their growth stage, at their soil moisture.

  4. iv.

    60% of Indian farmers don't read English or Hindi.

    Most "AI for farmers" apps stop existing the moment the language changes.

Act III · The Product

Three moves.
One conversation.

  1. Step 01

    Listen. Locate. Understand.

    Browser captures voice (or Twilio answers a phone call). Speech-to-Text in 22 languages, GPS pins the farm, Gemini extracts crop and growth stage from the conversation.

  2. Step 02

    Pull every signal. In parallel.

    One async fanout · four satellites, AgMarkNet's 112 commodities, Open-Meteo's 5-day forecast, Google Maps' driving distances. Cross-validated against each other before anything is said.

  3. Step 03

    Speak the answer back.

    Gemini 3 Flash synthesises the data into specific advice (irrigate / spray / sell / wait) in the farmer's language. Cloud TTS Wavenet voices it. Farmer can ask follow-ups.

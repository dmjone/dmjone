Breathing Techniques

Understanding the Breathing Animations: Each breathing technique features an animated colored bar that visually represents the breathing pattern. The bar expands to full width during the inhale phase, holds its width if there is a pause, and contracts during the exhale phase. This visual guide will help you synchronize your breathing with the animation for an effective practice. Adjust your breathing to match the movements of the bar - inhale as it expands, hold if it stays, and exhale as it contracts.

Box Breathing

(4-4-4-4 Technique)

Best for: Stress, Anxiety, Overwhelm

This technique is highly effective for calming the nervous system and promoting relaxation.

Follow these steps:

  1. Inhale Slowly: Breathe in quietly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  2. Hold Your Breath: Hold the breath for a count of 4 seconds.
  3. Exhale Slowly: Release the breath through your mouth for 4 seconds.
  4. Pause: Wait for another 4 seconds before your next breath.

Tips: Try to find a quiet, comfortable place to practice this technique. Ensure that your inhales and exhales are smooth and steady.

2:1 Breathing

(Inhale 4 seconds, Exhale 8 seconds)

Best for: Relaxation, Sleep Preparation

This technique promotes deep relaxation by activating the parasympathetic nervous system.

Here's how to do it:

  1. Inhale: Breathe in deeply through your nose for 4 seconds.
  2. Exhale: Exhale gently through your mouth for 8 seconds, focusing on a long, steady breath.

Tips: Perform this technique sitting or lying down to enhance relaxation.

5-5-5 Breathing

(Inhale 5 seconds, Hold 5 seconds, Exhale 5 seconds)

Best for: Focus, Concentration, Balance

Achieve a steady and focused mind with this balanced breathing method.

Follow these steps:

  1. Inhale: Inhale slowly and deeply through your nose for 5 seconds.
  2. Hold: Hold your breath for 5 seconds.
  3. Exhale: Exhale completely through your mouth for 5 seconds.

Tips: Ensure each part of the breathing cycle is smooth and controlled to maintain balance.

4-7-8 Breathing

(4 seconds Inhale, 7 seconds Hold, 8 seconds Exhale)

Best for: Anxiety, Insomnia

This technique can help reduce anxiety and aid sleep.

Here's how to practice it:

  1. Inhale: Inhale quietly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  2. Hold: Hold your breath for 7 seconds.
  3. Exhale: Exhale forcefully through your mouth, making a whoosh sound, for 8 seconds.

Tips: Best practiced before sleep as it promotes relaxation.

6-3-6-3 Breathing

(6 seconds Inhale, 3 seconds Hold, 6 seconds Exhale, 3 seconds Hold)

Best for: Centering, Balancing Emotions

Promote calmness and emotional balance with this technique.

  1. Inhale: Breathe in deeply through your nose for 6 seconds.
  2. Hold: Hold your breath for 3 seconds.
  3. Exhale: Slowly exhale through your mouth for 6 seconds.
  4. Hold: Pause for 3 seconds before the next breath.

Tips: This technique can be performed anytime you feel emotionally unbalanced.

4-4-8 Breathing

(4 seconds Inhale, 4 seconds Hold, 8 seconds Exhale)

Best for: Relaxation, Reducing Tension

This technique helps reduce tension and promote relaxation.

Here's what to do:

  1. Inhale: Inhale calmly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  2. Hold: Hold the breath for 4 seconds.
  3. Exhale: Exhale slowly and fully through your mouth for 8 seconds.

Tips: Try to perform in a quiet, comfortable setting to maximize relaxation.

1:2 Breathing

(Inhale 5 seconds, Exhale 10 seconds)

Best for: Deep Relaxation, Reducing Anxiety

Deepen your relaxation with this extended exhalation technique.

  1. Inhale: Inhale slowly and deeply through your nose for 5 seconds.
  2. Exhale: Exhale gently and fully through your mouth for 10 seconds.

Tips: Ideal for preparing for meditation or sleep due to its calming effects.

Alternate Nostril Breathing

(Nadi Shodhana)

Best for: Balancing, Clearing the Mind

Balance your mind and body with this focused breathing practice.

  1. Prepare: Sit in a comfortable position and use your right thumb to close your right nostril.
  2. Inhale: Inhale slowly through your left nostril.
  3. Switch: Close the left nostril with your fingers, then exhale through the right nostril.
  4. Continue: Inhale through the right nostril, switch, and exhale through the left.

Tips: This technique is great for reducing stress and enhancing concentration.

Diaphragmatic Breathing

(Belly Breathing)

Best for: Reducing Stress, Promoting Relaxation

Focus on deep, even breaths that expand the belly with this technique.

  1. Position: Lie on your back with knees slightly bent, or sit comfortably with your knees bent.
  2. Inhale: Place one hand on your belly. Inhale deeply through the nose, feeling your belly rise.
  3. Exhale: Exhale slowly through the mouth, feeling the belly lower.

Tips: This breathing is particularly effective for managing acute stress and promoting relaxation.
